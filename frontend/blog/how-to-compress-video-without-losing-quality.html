We've all been there: you try to email a video or send it on WhatsApp, but you get the dreaded "File size too large" error. Or worse, you send it, and it arrives looking like a pixelated mess.

Is it possible to significantly reduce file size while keeping the video looking sharp? Yes. The secret lies in using modern codecs.

Method 1: Convert to H.265 (HEVC)

Most standard videos use the H.264 codec. It's compatible but old. The newer H.265 (High Efficiency Video Coding) standard can deliver the same quality at 50% of the bit rate.

By simply converting your MP4 to H.265, you can cut the file size in half without any visible loss in quality.

Method 2: Use Intelligent Optimization

Tools like ConvertRocket don't just crudely chop bits. They use variable bit rate (VBR) encoding. This means the encoder uses more data for complex scenes (like an explosion) and less data for simple scenes (like a black screen or a still interview).

Method 3: Remove Unnecessary Audio Tracks

Did you know many MKV or MP4 files contain multiple audio tracks (different languages) or Surround Sound 5.1 data you don't need on your phone? Converting to a standard stereo AAC format can shave off megabytes.
