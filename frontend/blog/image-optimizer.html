Image Formats Explained: PNG vs JPG vs WebP

Published on Dec 19, 2025 • Design

Choosing the right image format can make or break your website's speed. Let's break down when to use which format.

JPG (JPEG)

Best for photographs with many colors. It uses lossy compression to keep file sizes very small.

PNG

Best for graphics, logos, and screenshots that need transparency or sharp edges. It's lossless but file sizes are larger.

WebP

The modern standard. WebP offers both lossy and lossless compression and is typically 26% smaller than PNGs and 25-34% smaller than JPEGs at equivalent quality.
