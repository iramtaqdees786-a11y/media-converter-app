Understanding PDF/A for Digital Archiving

Standard PDF files are not always suitable for long-term storage because they often rely on external fonts and external resources. PDF/A is an ISO-standardized version of PDF specialized for the digital preservation of electronic documents. Our laboratory ensures that all fonts are embedded and all external dependencies are internalized.

Why convert to PDF/A?

Many government agencies, legal firms, and libraries require documents in PDF/A format to ensure they will remain readable for decades to come, regardless of the software or fonts available on future systems. Our tool supports the PDF/A-1b and PDF/A-2b compliance levels.

Is my archived data safe?

The conversion process is executed on high-security computational nodes. We use enterprise-grade GhostScript configurations to ensure compliance. Once your PDF/A file is delivered, all temporary data is flushed from our laboratory's memory.
